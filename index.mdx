## How WiVRn works

WiVRn runs as an OpenXR runtime on your Linux PC. When your headset connects, it streams the rendered VR frames over your local network in real time — encoded using your GPU's hardware encoder for low latency and high quality.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the WiVRn server on your Linux PC">
    Available via native distro packages (Arch, Fedora, Gentoo, NixOS) or as a Flatpak on Flathub.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the WiVRn app on your headset">
    Available on the Meta Store for Quest headsets, or download the APK for Pico, HTC Vive, and others.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your headset to your PC">
    Open WiVRn on your headset. Your PC will appear in the server list — tap to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch a VR game on your PC">
    Start any OpenXR game. For Steam games using OpenVR, WiVRn automatically configures the compatibility layer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key features

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hardware video encoding" icon="microchip">
    Uses NVENC (Nvidia), VAAPI (AMD/Intel), or Vulkan Video for low-latency GPU-accelerated encoding. Software x264 is available as a fallback.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-codec support" icon="film">
    Stream using H.264, H.265 (HEVC), or AV1 — automatically negotiated between your PC and headset.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Steam & OpenVR compatibility" icon="gamepad-modern">
    Play Steam games that use OpenVR via xrizer or OpenComposite, which translate OpenVR calls to OpenXR transparently.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Automatic server discovery" icon="wifi">
    Your headset finds your PC automatically on the local network using mDNS/Avahi — no IP addresses to configure.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audio streaming" icon="volume">
    WiVRn creates a virtual audio device on your PC. Headset audio and microphone both stream over the connection.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dashboard GUI" icon="desktop">
    A Qt6 graphical dashboard handles setup, headset management, and server configuration — no command line required for day-to-day use.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
